About Dr. Kirby

Understanding How Humans Learn — From Every Angle

Dr. Angela Kirby believes that the best educators draw from behavioral, cognitive, and constructivist perspectives of teaching and learning. This belief isn't just a philosophy — it's the through line of a career spanning nearly three decades across every level of the education system.

As Director of PaTTAN's Harrisburg office since 2008, she supervises over 100 professional and support staff and manages a budget exceeding $35 million — leading statewide initiatives in reading, mathematics, autism, educator effectiveness, response to intervention, and more. Her deep expertise in federal funding streams and compliance ensures that resources are strategically deployed to maximize student impact.

Before PaTTAN, Dr. Kirby served as Special Assistant to the Pennsylvania Secretary of Education and Interim Acting Bureau Director at the Bureau of Teaching and Learning Support, where she helped develop Pennsylvania's Standards Aligned Systems. She has also been a coordinator of special education, a learning support and reading teacher, an inclusion specialist, and an adjunct professor at Temple University and Eastern University.

Now, Dr. Kirby is bringing that expertise to state departments of education, professional development and technical assistance centers, school districts, and families beyond Pennsylvania — helping them see learning through multiple lenses, navigate federal funding, and build systems where every student can thrive.

Consulting services rooted in how humans actually learn — drawing from behavioral, cognitive, and constructivist frameworks to create lasting change in classrooms, buildings, and systems.

Professional Development & Training

Expert navigation of IDEA, Section 504, and federal funding streams — helping state agencies, TA centers, and districts maximize resources, maintain compliance, and strategically deploy federal dollars to improve outcomes for students with disabilities.

Strategic Planning & Leadership Coaching

Help leaders build adaptive, learning-centered organizations — developing long-range plans, cultivating instructional decision-making capacity, and mentoring the next generation of special education leaders.

Family Engagement & Advocacy Support

Helping families understand how their child learns and how to advocate within the system — while consulting with organizations on building authentic family-school partnerships that honor every learner's unique profile.

Design learning environments that reflect how humans actually learn — multi-tiered frameworks, co-teaching models, universal design for learning, and accessible instructional materials grounded in multiple theoretical perspectives.
